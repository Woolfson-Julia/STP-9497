Welcome to Space Rush

Reflexes ready? Keep the star alive.

Play Now on Android

Space Rush is a fast-paced orbit survival game that will put your reaction speed to the ultimate test. Keep your shooting star circling smoothly, avoid sharp corners, collect celestial baits, and climb the score ladder across galaxies.

About

A Minimalist Rush Across the Stars

Inspired by the rhythm and precision of the classic Snake game, Space Rush challenges you to control a star through an endless orbital maze. The longer you survive, the faster it gets.

Its one-touch gameplay, sleek visual design, and ambient audio make it the perfect quick-play game—easy to start, hard to master. Whether you're waiting in line or winding down, Space Rush is your go-to time killer.

How to Play

Tap. Orbit. Survive.

Controlling the star is simple—tap to change its direction.

Your goal: stay inside the square orbit, avoid collisions at corners, and collect rotating baits to boost your score. The star will speed up over time, testing your reflexes and concentration.

Sounds easy? Just wait until you cross the 500-point mark. Only the most focused players make it to 1000 and beyond.

Game Modes & Difficulty

Start Simple. End Intense.

Space Rush begins with a slow, forgiving orbit. But every few seconds, your speed increases, and timing becomes critical.

There are no levels—just pure skill-based progression. How far can your star go before colliding with the edge?

Challenge your reflexes. Push your focus. It's not just a game. It's a rush.

FAQ

Yes, Space Rush is entirely free to download and play. There are no paywalls, no required in-app purchases, and no subscription fees. The core gameplay is fully accessible from the start, and all performance is based on your reflexes and focus. We believe in delivering a fair, skill-driven experience that rewards players based on ability—not spending. Optional ads may offer rewards, but they’re never intrusive. Our goal is to provide a clean, ad-light, user-friendly gameplay loop.

No, Space Rush is fully playable offline. You can enjoy the entire game without Wi-Fi or mobile data. This makes it perfect for travel, commutes, or areas with limited connectivity. Any high scores you achieve offline will be saved locally and synced to the leaderboard once your device reconnects. Events and minor updates will require internet, but core gameplay is always available.

The control system is intentionally simple: just tap the screen to change the direction of your shooting star. The game is built around one-thumb gameplay, so it's perfect for mobile use with minimal distractions. Timing is key—each tap changes your path along the orbit, and a single mistake could lead to a collision. As the speed increases, your reflexes and precision will be tested. It's easy to learn, but difficult to master.

Your main goal is to keep the star moving along its square orbit without crashing. Collect rotating baits that appear within the orbit to increase your score. As time passes, the speed of the orbit increases, demanding sharper reflexes and tighter control. Reaching 1000 points is considered a high-tier achievement. The longer you survive, the more galaxies you'll unlock through visual shifts and escalating challenge.

Baits are rotating collectibles that appear along your orbital path. Each one adds points to your total score. While it may seem easy at first, collecting them requires careful navigation around corners and increasing speed. Missing them isn't fatal, but collecting them boosts your progress and personal records. Some baits may trigger minor speed-ups or visual effects, so each one you collect adds both challenge and reward.

Yes, Space Rush features a global leaderboard where players can compete for the highest score. Scores are saved locally and synced online when your device connects to the internet. You can also see your personal best and track how your reflexes are improving over time. Weekly challenges and milestone records are planned for future updates. Compete against friends or strangers and prove your skills.

Absolutely. While the game starts at a relaxed pace, it gradually increases in difficulty the longer you survive. After 500 points, the difficulty curve steepens dramatically, testing both your muscle memory and timing. The game rewards patience, practice, and rhythm.
